Chapter 1

A Simple Network to Classify Digits

Break digit recognition into 2 subproblems:

  • Decide on which regions of an image the digits are in
  • Detect which digit is present in the chosen regions

We take the approach to focus on the image classification (step 2) first, as its the harder problem and its solution ensures we also have a good way to segment the digit regions as well.

Topology

We will use a 3-layer feed-forward neural network.

As we have training data in the form of \(28 \times 28\) grayscale images, our input layer has 784 neurons. We consider 0.0 to mean white and 1.0 means black.

Our output layer should encode digits, so it has 10 neurons.

And we take our intermediate layer to have variable dimension, to be tweaked as we see fit.

Definitions

  • Let \(x\) denote a 748-D vector from the training set
  • \(y = y(x)\) is the desired 10-D output vector determined by the true value of the \(x\) digit.
  • \(a\) is the computed output vector of the network based on the input \(x\).

The cost function is schematically given by:

$$ C(W,b) \equiv \frac{1}{2n} \sum_x \| y(x) - a \|^2 $$
