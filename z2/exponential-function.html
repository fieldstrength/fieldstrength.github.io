exponential function

General Definition

The exponential map is a function from a Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\) to its corresponding Lie group \(G\).

$$ \exp : \mathfrak{g} \to G $$

It is a group homomorphism.

We understand the standard definition as the special case where

  • \(\mathfrak{g}\) is the group \(\{\mathbb{R}, +, 0\}\), the real numbers under addition.
  • \(G\) is the group \(\{ \mathbb{R}^+, \times, 1 \}\), the positive reals under multiplication.

Basic definition

The exponential function is formed by raising the real number \(e\) to the power of the input.

$$ \exp(z) = e^z $$

It can be defined in terms of the power series

$$ \exp(z) \equiv \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{z^n}{n!} $$

which in turn furnishes a definition of \(e\). This definition works equally well for real numbers, complex numbers, or matrices.

Properties

Algebraic properties

Important equational properties are encoded in describing the exponential function as a group homomorphism from the reals under addition to the positive reals under multiplication.

One identity is mapped to the other identity.

$$ e^0 = 1 $$

And group multiplication (addition) maps to group multiplication (times).

$$ e^{x+y} = e^x e^y $$

It is furthermore an isomorphism as it has an inverse, also a group homomorphism.

$$ \ln(xy) = \ln(x) + \ln(y) $$

$$ \ln(1) = 0 $$

Dynamical properties

The exponential function is noteworthy for being unchanged under the derivative.

$$ \frac{d}{dz} e^z = e^z $$

Relation to arbitrary exponents

$$ z^w = e^{w \ln z} $$
