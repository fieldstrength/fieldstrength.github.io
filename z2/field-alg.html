field

A field is a ring together with some operation like “division”. [EXPAND ME]

not to be confused with: the field physical concept.

Links to this page
  • tensor

    Definition. A type (n,m)-tensor over the field \(k\) is a mapping from \(n\) elements of the cotangent space and \(m\) of the tangent space to \(k\) that is linear in each of these inputs.

  • matrix

    Every type (size) of matrix \(n,m\) over a particular field is a vector space in exactly the same way that a size \(n \times m\) column vector is.

    We can have matrices over an arbitrary field. We will speak of matrices over [real](R)❌ or complex numbers with the possible generalization being implicit.

  • algebra

    An algebra over a field \(K\) is a vector space over \(K\) equipped with a bilinear product.

  • Mat

    Definition. \(\mathrm{Mat}_f\) is the category whose objects are natural numbers and whose arrows are matrices in the field \(f\), where arrow composition is matrix multiplication, and a matrix of signature \((n,m)\) goes from the object \(n\) to the object \(m\).
