scientific theory

In the philsophy of science, the scientific theory is the artifact that produces predictions given certain input data together with ambient contextual assumptions.

In practice the way predictions are produced can be refined by distinguishing the specific scientific model from the overarching scientific framework.

Opinion: Confusion around this point is responsible for popular bad takes about the philosophy of science, the status of fundamental physics and certain scientific theories in particular.

caution about terminology

Although this concept is fundamental to science, it does not at all correspond to how scientists, or at least physicists, use the word “theory”. Instead this term usually refers either to something in mathematics or to a scientific framework. Examples: twistor theory, M-theory, quantum field theory.

Links to this page
  • string theory

    String theory, notably, functions both as a scientific framework as well as the specific scientific theory.

  • scientific model

    A scientific model, by my definition, is a scientific framework together with additional data needed to make its predictions fully precise in order to constitute a scientific theory.

  • scientific framework

    For example, string theory is both a scientific framework and a scientific theory, in the sense that different vacua are not completely disconnected propositions about nature because dynamical transitions between them are actually possible. At least a given superselection sector (i.e choice of asymptotic boundary conditions).

    In the service of constructing a scientific theory, a framework means the general mathematical structure, which must be further supplemented by phenomenologically motivated data in order to be ready to make useful predictions.

  • quantum mechanics

    It is noteworthy for departing from its predecessor in not being deterministic (at least in practice), but rather giving predictions only in the form of probability of allowed outcome. This was a radical departure from the expectations that were traditionally placed on a scientific theory or scientific framework.

    While we know for sure that our current scientific theorys based on QM are incomplete, notably lacking an account of quantum gravity, it is not yet clear if the framework of QM itself must be modified or superceded. Notably string theory is a singularly promising candidate framework for quantum gravity that does not modify QM itself.

  • landscape

    The landscape is the solution set for a physical scientific theory, the set of distinct ways it can manifest in the world. In particular, it generally means a set of vacua – local energy minima – in whose vicinity all known history has occurred.

  • fine tuning

    When unfixed parameters of a scientific theory have values that do not appear to be sampled from the expected distrubtion. For example a real number being extremely close to zero, or two numbers being very close together.
