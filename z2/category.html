category

definition

Definition. A category is

  • A collection of objects.

  • A collection of morphisms – or arrows – between objects.

  • For any morphism f there is a domain and codomain object. We write \(f : X \to Y\) to signify that \(X\) is the domain and \(Y\) is the codomain.

  • For every object X a designated identity morphism \(1_X : X \to X\)

  • Composition of morphisms. Given

    • \(f : A \to B\)

    • \(g : B \to C\)

      We can form the compound morphism \(g \circ f : A \to C\)

  • The identity morphism is, as the name implies, the identity on either the left or right.

    Given \(f : A \to B\) then: $$ 1_B \circ f = f = f \circ 1_A $$

  • Composition is associative. $$ (h \circ g) \circ f = h \circ (g \circ f) $$

categories big and small

Small

Definition. A category is small if its arrows form a set.

Note that since the objects are 1-to-1 with the identity arrows, this means the objects form a set too.

Locally small

Definition. A category is locally small if the arrows between any pair of objects form a set.

We write \(\mathrm{hom}(X,Y)\) to represent the set of morphisms from X to Y, the homset, in a locally small category.

Examples

  • Set – Sets and functions between them
  • Top – Topological spaces and continuous functions between them
  • Rel – Sets and relations
  • Hilb – Hilbert spaces and bounded linear maps between them.
  • \(\mathbf{Vect}_K\) – Vector spaces over the field \(K\) and linear maps between them
  • Monoids and monoid homomorphisms
  • Groups and group homomorphisms
