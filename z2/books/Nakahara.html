Nakahara now defines the simplicial chain complex but calls this simply the “chain complex” Lemma 3.3 proves that it is indeed a chain complex.

The r-boundaries are a subset of the r-cycles which are a subset of the r-chains. (Theorem 3.3)

$$ B_r(K) \subset Z_r(K) \subset C_r(K) $$

they are defined in terms of the boundary-operator

$$ Z_r(K) \equiv \ker \partial_r $$

$$ B_r(K) \equiv \mathrm{im} \partial_{r+1} $$

and their quotient space is the homology group.

$$ H_r(K) \equiv Z_r(K)/B_r(K) = \ker \partial_r / \mathrm{im} \partial_{r+1} $$

This turns out to be isomorphic (skipping to chapter 6) to the de Rham cohomology group.

$$ H^r(K) \equiv Z^r(K)/B^r(K) = \ker d_{r+1} / \mathrm{im} d_r $$
