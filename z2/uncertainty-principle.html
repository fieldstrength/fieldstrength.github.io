Heisenberg uncertainty principle

The combined uncertainties (variances) of two different observables \(A,B\) is bounded from below by proportional to the expectation value of their commutator.

$$ \bbox[8px,border:2px solid #DAB5E5]{ \sigma_A^2 \sigma_B^2 \geq \frac{1}{2i} \left \langle [A,B] \right \rangle } $$

In particular for the case of position and momentum we have

$$ \sigma_x^2 \sigma_p^2 \geq \frac{\hbar}{2} $$

Proof

Let \(\Psi\) be our quantum state. For an observable \(A\) define the state

$$ \left \lvert f \right \rangle = (\hat{A} - \mathbb{I} \left \langle A \right \rangle) \left \lvert \Psi \right \rangle $$

This state is constructed such that its norm is the variance of \(A\).

$$ \sigma_A^2 = \left \langle f \vert f \right \rangle $$

And similarly construct the state \(\left \lvert g \right \rangle\) for the variance of \(B\). Now invoke the Schwarz inequality.

$$ \sigma_A^2 \sigma_B^2 = \left \langle f \vert f \right \rangle \left \langle g \vert g \right \rangle \geq \Big | \left \langle f \vert g \right \rangle \Big|^2 $$

Now we note that the imaginary part of any complex number \(z\) can be written

$$ \Im(z) = \frac{1}{2i} (z-z^\star) $$

$$ \begin{aligned} \left \langle f \vert g \right \rangle &= \langle \Psi \vert (\hat A - \left \langle A \right \rangle )(\hat B - \left \langle B \right \rangle ) \vert\Psi \rangle \\ &= \langle \Psi \vert (AB - A\langle B \rangle - B \langle A \rangle + \langle A \rangle \langle B \rangle ) \vert\Psi \rangle \\ &= \langle AB \rangle - \langle A \rangle \langle B \rangle - \langle B \rangle \langle A \rangle + \langle A \rangle \langle B \rangle \\ &= \langle AB \rangle - \langle B \rangle \langle A \rangle \end{aligned} $$

So we can compute the difference of the two inner product combinations. $$ \langle f \vert g \rangle - \langle g \vert f \rangle = \langle AB \rangle - \langle BA \rangle = \langle [A,B] \rangle $$
