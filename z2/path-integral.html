path integral

The path integral is an alternative formulation to [quantum](qm)❌ dynamics from the usual operator formalism.

An especially appealing property is that it allows expressing unitary evolution with manifest Poincaré group spacetime symmetry.

Derivation for 1D quantum particle

We will utilize the group property of the unitary evolution operators, to decompose it and insert identities.

We want to compute a general time evolution in the Heisenberg picture. $$ \langle x_F, t_F \vert x_I, t_I \rangle $$

We divide the time evolution operator \(N\) times, yielding \(N+1\) piecees. So we define

$$ T \equiv t_F - t_I $$

$$ \delta t = \frac{T}{N+1} $$

$$ \langle x_F, t_F \vert x_I, t_I \rangle = \int \prod_{j=1}^N $$

$$ U(t_1)U(t_2) = U(t_1 + t_2) $$

And the completeness of the position observable.

$$ \sum_x \lvert x \rangle \langle x \rvert = \mathbb{I} $$

First use the group property to decompose the time evolution operator at an arbitrary intermediate time point.

$$ U(t_1, t_2) = U(t_1, t_\alpha) \ \mathbb{I} \ U(t_\alpha, t_2) $$

And now use the completeness relation for the spatial eigenstate \(\lvert x \rangle\)

$$ U(t_1, t_2) = \int dx_ U(t_1, t_\alpha) \ \mathbb{I} \ U(t_\alpha, t_2) $$

  • The Perturbative Approach to Path Integrals: A Succinct Mathematical Treatment

Quantum field theory often makes use of manipulations of path integrals that are withouta proper mathematical definition and hence have only a formal meaning. These computations are usually presented in a manner that makes it difficult to ascertain the mathematicalnature of these operations, other than that they are inspired from familiar properties of finite-dimensional integrals. The purpose of this paper is to explain in a large class of important examples how formal path integral manipulations constitute notation for otherwise mathematically well-defined procedures.
