string theory

Otherwise known as M-theory. “String theory” is the most recognized name for the one scientific framework that grew out of models of quantum mechanical strings.

Strings represent a natural way that gravity could be [unified](unification)❌ with [particle physics](qft)❌, as gravity is a necessary consequence of strings in the presence of quantum mecahnics. String theory is the only known theory of theory of

Summary

Construction

The theories are based around the perturbation theory of [quantum](qm)❌ [supersymmetric](susy)❌ strings. Which come in 5 different forms. These string theories are related to each other and to 11-dimensional supergravity by dualitys.

Philosophy of Science considerations

String theory, notably, functions both as a scientific framework as well as the specific scientific theory.

In practice string theory is a framework like quantum field theory.

Mathematical formulation

Classical string worldsheet

Kinematics

Start with the worldsheet.

$$ \Sigma = \mathbb{R}\times [0, 2\pi) $$

$$ \begin{pmatrix} \sigma^0 \\ \sigma^1 \\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \tau \\ \sigma \\ \end{pmatrix} $$

Following Tong, its nice to define the tension

$$ T = \frac{1}{2\pi \alpha'} $$

Alternatively, \(\alpha'\) is the square root of the string length scale

$$ l_s^2 = \alpha' $$

Polyakov action

$$ S = -\frac{T}{2} \int d^2 \sigma \sqrt{-g} g^{\alpha \beta} \partial_\alpha X^\mu \partial_\beta X^\nu \eta_{\mu \nu} $$

We have a stress energy tensor

$$ \bbox[8px,border:2px solid #DAB5E5]{ T_{\alpha \beta} = -\frac{2}{T} \frac{1}{\sqrt{g}} \frac{\partial S}{\partial g^{\alpha \beta}} } $$

Symmetries

  • Under the Poincaré group in the target spacetime.
  • Reparametrization invariance
  • Weyl invariance

Gauge-fixing

Gauge-fix the metric to the flat Minkowskian metric

$$ g_{\alpha \beta} = \eta_{\alpha \beta} $$

And we get the simplified action:

$$ S = -\frac{T}{2} \int d^2 \sigma \partial_\alpha X \cdot \partial^\alpha X $$

and free wave equations for \(X\):

$$ \partial_\alpha \partial^\alpha X^\mu = 0 $$

While the variation of the metric forces our stress-energy-tensor to vanish.

$$ T_{01} = \partial_\tau X \cdot \partial_\sigma X = 0 $$

$$ T_{00} = T_{11} = \tfrac{1}{2}(\partial_\tau X + \partial_\sigma X) = 0 $$

Mode expansion

lightcone coordinates:

$$ \sigma^\pm = \tau \pm \sigma $$

Rephrased EQOM

$$ \partial_+ \partial_{-} X^\mu = 0 $$

The general solution is

$$ X^\mu (\sigma, \tau) = X_L^\mu(\sigma^+) + X_R^\mu(\sigma^-) $$
