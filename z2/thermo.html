Thermodynamics, and its precise incarnations in [statistical mechanics], concern systems whose number of degrees of freedom is so high that qualitatively new effects emerge, like for example [temperature].

It is noteworthy for the way that the same statements, with similar consequencess, hold for all kinds of different physical systems. As thermodynamics has inherently to do with Information, it is very widely applicable rather than being opinionated about the form of physical system involved.
