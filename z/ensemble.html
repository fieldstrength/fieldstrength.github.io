ensemble

An ensemble is a quantitative model of the probabilities of the states of a system.

Definition. An ensemble \(X\) is a triple \((x, \mathcal{A}_X, \mathcal{P}_X)\) where

  • x is the outcome
  • \(\mathcal{A}_X = \big\{ a_1 ... a_N \big\}\) the set of allowed values of x.
  • \(\mathcal{P}_X = \big\{ p_1 ... p_N \big\} = \big\{ P(x=a_1) ... P(x=a_N) \big\}\) the probabilities of the outcomes.

To have a consistent probability interpretation the \(p_x\) must satisfy

$$ 0 \leq p_i \leq 1 $$

$$ \sum_{a_i \in A_X} p_i = 1 $$

Basic definitions

joint ensemble

Definition. A Joint Ensemble \(XY\) is an ensemble whose state-space is an ordered pair, namely a cartesian product.

$$ \mathcal{A}_{(X,Y)} = X \times Y $$

The probabilities \(P(x,y)\) are called joint probabilities.

Note that the probabilities are not generally independent

$$ P(x,y) \neq P(x)P(y) $$

marginal probability

Definition. The marginal probability is the probability of an outcome from one of the subensembles. It’s found by summing over the other random variable(s).

$$ P(x=a_i) \equiv \sum_{y \in A_Y} P(x=a_i, y) $$

conditional probability

Definition. The conditional probability is “the probability that \(x=a_i\) given \(y=b_j\)”

$$ P(x=a_i | y=b_j) \equiv \frac{P(x=a_i,y=b_j)}{P(y=b_j)} $$

assuming of course that \(P(y=b_j) \neq 0\).

implications

Here we’ll let \(\mathcal{H}\) denote some assumption upon which we base our probabilistic analysis.

product rule

is obtained from the definition of conditional probability.

$$ \begin{aligned} P(x,y | \mathcal{H}) &= P(x|y,\mathcal{H}) \\ &= P(y|x,\mathcal{H})P(x|\mathcal{H}) \end{aligned} $$

sum rule

is obtained by rewriting the marginal probability:

$$ \begin{aligned} P(x|\mathcal{H}) &= \sum_y P(x,y|\mathcal{H}) \\ &= \sum_y P(x|y, \mathcal{H}) P(y|\mathcal{H}) \end{aligned} $$
