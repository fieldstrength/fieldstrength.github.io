Lorentz group

The Lorentz group is the orthogonal group O(3,1).

It is the group that preserves

$$ \eta_{\mu\nu}x^\mu x^\nu = t^2 - x^2 - y^2 - z^2 $$

We’ll describe the action of the group in terms of the matrix \(\Lambda\).

$$ x^\mu \to \Lambda^\mu_\nu x^\nu $$

Expressing the preservation another way:

$$ \eta_{\mu\nu}x^\mu x^\nu = \eta_{\rho \sigma}y^\rho y^\sigma \equiv \eta_{\mu \nu} (\Lambda^\mu_\rho x^\rho) (\Lambda^\nu_\sigma x^\sigma) $$

$$ \eta_{\rho \sigma} = \eta_{\mu \nu}\Lambda^\mu_\rho \Lambda^\nu_\sigma $$

$$ \eta = \Lambda^T \eta \Lambda $$

Taking the determinant of this equation

$$ (\det \Lambda)^2 = 1 \implies \det \Lambda = \pm 1 $$

The \(\det \Lambda = 1\) subset are the proper Lorentz transformations \(SO(3,1)\). This is a subgroup.

The \(\det \Lambda = -1\) subset are improper Lorentz transformations. They are not a subgroup. They can always be formed by a discrete transformation together with a proper Lorentz transformation.

Infinitessimal form

Now consider the infinitessimal form

$$ {\Lambda^\mu}_\nu = \delta^\mu_\nu + {\omega^\mu}_\nu $$

The matrix equation above implies [PROVE ME] that \(\omega\) must be antisymmetric

$$ \omega_{\mu\nu} = - \omega_{\nu\mu} $$

An antisymmetric 4x4 matrix has \(\frac{16-4}{2} = 6\) parameters.

Rotations

$$ \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \to \begin{pmatrix} x \cos \theta + y \sin \theta \\ y \cos \theta - x \sin \theta \\ \end{pmatrix} $$

Boosts

$$ \begin{pmatrix} t \\ x \end{pmatrix} \to \begin{pmatrix} \gamma (t + vx) \\ \gamma (x + vt) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} (\cosh \eta)t + (\sinh \eta) x \\ (\sinh \eta)t + (\cosh \eta) x \\ \end{pmatrix} $$

where

$$ v = \tanh \eta $$

$$ \gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-v^2}} = \cosh \eta $$

Representations

In finite form the transformation reads

$$ \Lambda = \exp \Big[ - \tfrac{i}{2} \omega_{\mu \nu} J^{\mu \nu} \Big] $$

in general there are matrix indices attached to the \(\Lambda\)s that are not necessarily Lorentz indices themselves.

Scalar representation

This quantity is invariant, not changing at all under the transformations.

Vector representations

  • Contravariant 4-vector transformation: \(V^\mu \to {\Lambda^\mu}_\nu V^\nu\)
  • Covariant 4-vector transformation: \(V_\mu \to {\Lambda_\mu}^\nu V_\nu\)

The generators then have Lorentz group indices: $$ {(J^{\mu \nu})^\rho}_\sigma = i(\eta^{\mu \rho} \delta^\nu_\sigma - \eta^{\nu \rho} \delta^\mu_\sigma) $$

$$ \bbox[8px,border:2px solid #DAB5E5]{ [J^{\mu\nu},J^{\rho, \sigma}] = i(\eta^{\nu \rho}J^{\mu \sigma} - \eta^{\mu \rho} J^{\nu \sigma} - \eta^{\nu \sigma}J^{\mu \rho} + \eta^{\mu \sigma}J^{\nu \rho}) } $$

the Lie algebra of \(SO(3,1)\).
