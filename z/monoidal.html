Definitions

monoidal category

A monoidal category is a #category \(C\) and the data:

  • A functor: \(\otimes : C \times C \to C\), the tensor product.
  • An object \(I \in C\) the unit object.
  • Natural isomorphisms \(\alpha_{A,B,C} : (A \otimes B) \otimes C \to A \otimes (B \otimes C)\), the associators for all categories \(A,B,C \in C\)
  • Natural isomorphisms \(\lambda_A : I \otimes A \to A\), the left unitors for all categories \(A \in C\)
  • Natural isomorphisms \(\rho_A : A \otimes I \to A\) the right unitors for all categories \(A \in C\)
  • The coherence property. Usually given as the triangle and pentagon identities. Put another way:

The coherence property is that every well-formed equation built from \(◦, ⊗, id, α, α^{−1}, λ, λ^{−1}, ρ\) and \(ρ^{−1}\) is satisfied.

Interchange law

Theorem (interchange). $$ (g \circ f) \otimes (j \circ h) = (g \otimes j) \circ (f \otimes h) $$

Proof. $$ \begin{aligned} (g \circ f) \otimes (j \circ h) &= \bigotimes(g \circ f,j \circ h) \\ &= \bigotimes ((g,j) \circ (f,h)) \\ &= \bigotimes (g,j) \circ \bigotimes(f,h) \\ &= (g \otimes j) \circ (f \otimes h) \end{aligned} $$

This proof proceeds first by writing the tensor product as a functor on the product category, then invoking the definition of composition of the product \(\times\), then by using the functoriality of \(\bigotimes\), then restoring the operator notation.

Graphical calculus

So even for ordinary categories, the graphical calculus is extremely useful: it somehow “absorbs” our axioms, making them a consequence of the notation. This is because the axioms of a category have something to do with the topology of space at a fundamental level — in particular, 1-dimensional manifolds. Of course, this graphical representation isn’t so unfamiliar. We usually call it algebra.

– Introduction to Categorical Quantum Mechanics, Chris Heunen and Jamie Vicary

Examples

  • Set# the category of sets and functions, with tensor product given by Cartensian product.
  • Vect# the category of vector spaces and linear maps, with tensor product the Kronecker product

Refinements

braided monoidal category

Definition. A braided monoidal category is a monoidal category C with:

  • A natural isomorphism, the braiding $$ b_{X,Y} : X \otimes Y \to Y \otimes X $$

Such that two diagrams, the hexagon identities, must commute.

Symmetric monoidal category

Definition. A symmetric monoidal category is a braided monoidal category whose braiding satisfies

$$ B_{y,x} \circ B_{x,y} = \mathrm{Id}_{x \otimes y} $$

Closed monoidal category

Definition. A symmetric monoidal category is closed if: \(\forall b \in C_0\)

The “arrow-from-\(b\)” functor is right adjoint to the “tensor-product with \(b\)” functor.

$$ [b,-] : C \to C \ \ \dashv \ \ (- \otimes b) : C \to C $$
